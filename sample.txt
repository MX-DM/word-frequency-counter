A typical gasoline car engine explodes gasoline to make it spin.

Inside an engine are pistons that move up and down inside a cylinder.
As the engine turns the pistons first move down to suck in a mixture of air fuel, then move back up to compress it.

The fuel is then ignited by a spark plug and this explosion forces the piston back down and creates power to spin the engine.
The piston then moves back up pushing the burned fuel or exhaust out of the cylinder and the cycle repeats.

Different cylinders are timed so that one is exploding while another is compressing.
The pistons are all connected the the crank shaft which spins the pistons and is driven by the explosions.
The crank shaft is attached to your transmission which in turn drives the wheels.

At the top of cylinders, called the cylinder head, are valves that open and close to let fuel and air in, and exhaust out at the right times.
This is driven by a camshaft, which is in turn driven by the crankshaft via a chain or gears.
